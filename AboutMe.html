About

Soldier Rob

My career started with the United States Army back in May 2001. Yes, that was just months before 9/11,
and that changed how I thought being in the Army was going to be like. I first deployed to Afghanistan in early 2002,
and stayed there until the following year. This deployment was exciting and new and was relatively quiet compared
to my second deployment in 2006-2007. On the second deployment my 12-person team was charged with securing an area that was
75 miles wide and 150 miles long in Southern Afghanistan along the Pakistani border.

Since then, I have served the United States in various roles, and have lived and worked in Africa, Europe and Central America.
Above is a picture of myself training the 12th Kenyan Engineers prior to their invasion of Southern Somalia in 2011.

After the mission in Africa I attended Queen’s University Belfast in Belfast Northern Ireland and earned a master’s degree
in Violence, Terrorism, and Security. Following my graduation I was set to take a job near the annexed region of Ukraine,,br> but life intervened.

So, I moved back to Salt Lake City to be with my family, and began working in Emergency Medical Services (EMS).
This is a rewarding job, but it comes with long hours and not great pay. In search of something with better hours and pay
I started helping a friend promote his websites.

For the last few years I have been learning all about digital marketing and search engine optimization.
I have found the world wide web a very interesting and limitless adventure, and that is how I ended up in this class
learning how to code and develop websites.
